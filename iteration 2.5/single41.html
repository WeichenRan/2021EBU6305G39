    Chronicle

    • Dynasty: Qin
    • Category: bamboo slips
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Unearthed: Yunmeng County, Hubei Province

    There are 53 bamboo slips, also known as "memorabilia". The text of the bamboo slips is an ink Book Qin Li, with a total of 550 words. Most of the handwriting is clear and distinguishable. It is divided into upper and lower columns. It describes the war process of unifying the country from 306 BC (the first year of King Zhao of Qin) to 217 BC (the 30th year of emperor Shi Huang of Qin) and the happy life and related matters of the tomb owner. The bamboo slips were originally untitled. The main content is that they record year by year the major events such as the wars that Qin unified China from the first year of King Zhaoxiang of Qin Dynasty (306 BC) to the 33rd year of emperor Shihuang of Qin Dynasty (217 BC). Some are consistent with the historical books, and some can make up for the lack of historical books. In addition, some similar chronological data are recorded. The bamboo slips of "Bian Nian" provide valuable materials for studying the military, politics and calendar timing of the Qin Dynasty. It is the original of the earliest history book preserved so far.
