    Compendium of Materia Medica

    • Dynasty: Ming
    • Category: Books
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Author: Li Shizhen

    This is a great work of pharmacology in the Ming Dynasty, which has made great contributions to plant taxonomy. Author Li Shizhen (1518-1593 AD), from Jizhou. He inherited his father's practice of medicine, compiled the compendium of Materia Medica since the 31st year of Jiajing (AD 1552), read more than 800 kinds of medical works, combined with his own knowledge and experience, experienced three major modifications, and finally completed this work in the sixth year of Wanli (AD 1578).

    The book is divided into 16 parts, 62 categories and 50 volumes, including 1892 kinds of Chinese herbal medicines, 11096 prescriptions and 1110 illustrations, vividly showing the complex forms and effects of various drugs. The book makes a scientific classification of the drugs contained in it.
