    New youth

    • Dynasty: Modern
    • Category: Books
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Size: 26cm long 18cm wide

    Youth magazine was founded in Shanghai on September 15, 1915. Monthly. Issue 6 is a volume. It was renamed "New Youth" from Volume II. In the early stage, it was mainly to publicize democracy and science, oppose feudal autocracy and superstition, and attack the feudal cultural thought represented by Confucius and Mencius. After the October Revolution, Russia began to publicize the October Revolution and introduce Marxism. Since the eighth volume in September 1920, it has become the organ publication of the Shanghai Communist group and has waged a fierce struggle against various schools of anti Marxist thought of the bourgeoisie and petty bourgeoisie. After the founding of the Communist Party of China, it once became the party's organ newspaper. It was suspended in February 1927. In June 1923, it was changed into a quarterly journal and became a theoretical Journal of the CPC Central Committee. Since April 1925, it has published indefinite periodicals. It was closed in July 1926. This publication spread widely in China at that time and had a great influence.

    The foreign language on the cover of this issue of youth magazine is French, namely "Youth". The avatar is Andrew Carnegie (1835-1919), the "steel king" who finally donated wealth to society in the United States. 1915 is the 80th anniversary of Carnegie's birthday. This journal is echoed by Peng dezun's article "the winner of hard work: a biography of Carnegie". Important articles in this issue also include Chen Duxiu's "warning youth" (the publication of the magazine), "French people and modern civilization", "women's view" and "history of modern civilization"; Gao Yihan wrote the Republic and the consciousness of youth; Wang Shuqian wrote "old and new problems"; Chen Peng wrote "spring tide" and so on.
