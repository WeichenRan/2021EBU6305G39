    Yuhou copper seal

    • Dynasty: Song
    • Category: bronze ware
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Unearthed: Unrecorded

    The bronze seal in the distance is one of the official seals of the forbidden army. In the early years of the Northern Song Dynasty, Taizu of the Song Dynasty dispatched local elite soldiers to supplement the forbidden army and stationed in the capital and nearby areas for many years, which greatly weakened the strength of the local army and was unable to compete with the central government. In order to strengthen the direct control over the forbidden army, the highest military position is not set. The Department in front of the hall is in charge of the commander of the capital, the bodyguard department is in charge of the commander of the horse army and the commander of the infantry army respectively, and the three commanders are appointed by the emperor, known as the "three yamen". "Gongsheng" is one of the troops under the jurisdiction of the bodyguard Sima army. It was established by Emperor Taizong of the Song Dynasty. It has 21 commanders (each command establishment is 500 people) stationed in the capital. Duyu Hou is the deputy chief of this army.
