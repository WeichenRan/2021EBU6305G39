    translation of natural selection

    • Dynasty: Modern
    • Category: Books
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Size: 22.7cm long and 25.5cm wide

    This is Yan Fu's translation of the manuscript of the theory of heavenly evolution by Huxley, a British biologist. The original name of "evolution and ethics" is a collection of Huxley's papers, which publicizes Darwin's theory of evolution. Yan Fuxuan translated the first two of them and renamed them Tianyan Lun. Yan Fu's translation is not a pure literal translation, but adds his own comments. Based on Huxley's theory of biological evolution, such as "natural selection, survival of the fittest", he expounded the viewpoint of saving the nation from subjugation and survival, called on the people to "win with heaven", take the road of enriching the country and strengthening the army, and change the passive situation of backwardness and being beaten, which caused great shock in the ideological circle at that time and had a positive and far-reaching impact. Kang Youwei, the leader of the reformists, praised Yan Fu for "not seeing such a person in his eyes" after seeing the translation, saying that Yan Fu "translated Tianyan Lun as the first of China's western learning".
