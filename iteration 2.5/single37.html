    Pointed stone tool

    • Dynasty: Paleolithic Age
    • Category: stone tools
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Unearthed: Dingcun site,Shanxi Province

    The large triangular pointed instrument is relatively thick and is made of huge angular shale thick stone flakes, with a triangular cross section. Because of its unique shape and production technology, it is regarded by scholars as a distinctive artifact in Dingcun culture, which may be a tool for excavating rhizome plants. These two artifacts are typical large triangular pointed artifacts from the Dingcun site.

    The Paleolithic features of Dingcun are distinct, but scholars have different understanding of its cultural tradition. One holds that Dingcun is a typical representative of the Paleolithic system in North China; The other believes that Dingcun is just a large stone culture that suddenly appeared in the middle of the Paleolithic age in North China, and its context is not clear.
