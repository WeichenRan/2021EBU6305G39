    imperial jade seal

    • Dynasty: Han
    • Category: Jade
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Unearthed: Xianyang City, Shaanxi Province

    White jade, with dark brown Qin. Square, covered with bucket shaped button, with a pair of transverse perforations on both sides of the uppermost end, which can be tied. The printing surface is also square, and the seal character "Huaiyang King seal" is engraved on it. The characters are exquisitely carved, the fonts are correct and smooth, the lines are powerful, and the characters are neatly arranged. It is a fine product of Han official seal and has high historical and artistic value. It also has a high position in the history of calligraphy and is an important material material for the study of seal cutting in the Han Dynasty.

    This seal is the official seal of Han Dynasty. In the history of the Han Dynasty, the king of Huaiyang had five people in three departments: Liu you, the son of Gaozu, Liu Wu, the son of Emperor Wen, and Liu Qin, the son of Emperor Xuan. According to the characteristics of the printing form and text, it should be the first seal of Emperor Xuan's son Liu Qin. According to the list of princes and kings in the book of Han Dynasty, "Liu Qin was established as the king of Huaiyang in the third year of Yuankang and passed on to the third generation." According to the old instrument of the Han Dynasty, the seal of the princes of the Western Han Dynasty is a gold seal, a date camel button, and a king's seal. The texture and button style of this seal are different from those recorded. The use of official seal at that time can be taught by those who inherit it from generation to generation. It was printed in 1959 by the National Palace Museum.

    After Qin Shihuang unified China, it was stipulated that the seals of the emperor, Empress and princes were called seals, and generally they were called seals and seals. The Han Dynasty adopted the Qin Dynasty system, and this jade seal is consistent with the above customization. In 1968, a jade seal of the "Queen's seal" of the Han Dynasty was unearthed beside the langjiagou canal in Hanjiawan commune, Xianyang, Shaanxi Province. The jade is white and moist and square. With a height of 2 cm and a side length of 2.8 cm, it is the only emperor and empress seal of the Han Dynasty found at present. It is a treasure in the Han seal.
