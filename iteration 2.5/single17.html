    Tongxing banknote

    • Dynasty: Ming
    • Category: Currency
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Unearthed: multi places

    This Daming pass treasure note is made of mulberry paper. It is 22 cm long and 13.5 cm wide. Cyan gray, household edition, the horizontal book on the forehead is "Daming pass treasure banknote", "Ming" is small, "Bao" is large, and the text stroke is slightly thick. The lower and outer part is a fence composed of dragon patterns and tangled flowers, which is divided into upper and lower parts. The upper central horizontal book "Sishi Wen" is decorated with 40 coins under the face value, which is distributed horizontally. The seal characters on both sides are "Daming treasure note" and "universal". There are imperial edicts in the lower part. There are two red official seals on the face of the note. The upper part is "the seal of Daming treasure note" and the lower part is "the seal of treasure note lifting Department". The ink is seriously decolorized. There is a square ink seal on the upper part of the back, which is "printed by the Bureau of making treasure notes", and the seal is gorgeous and clear. The lower rectangular flower column shows the face value and money figure. Hubu version of small denomination Daming pass banknotes was printed in the 22nd year of Hongwu (1389).
