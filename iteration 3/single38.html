    Colored pottery basin

    • Dynasty: Neolithic Age
    • Category: Pottery
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Unearthed: Banpo site, Shaanxi Province

    The painted pottery basin is red, the mouth edge is painted with intermittent black ribbons, and the inner wall is painted with black to draw two groups of symmetrical human face fish patterns. The human face is round, and the top of the head is decorated with tips like a bun and fins. The right half of the forehead is painted black, and the left half is black and half arc. The eyes are thin and straight, like closed eyes. The bridge of the nose is straight and in an inverted "t" shape. There is a deformed fish pattern on the left and right sides of the mouth. The fish head coincides with the outline of the human mouth. It seems that there are two big fish in the mouth at the same time. In addition, there are two opposite small fish on the human face and ears, which form a mermaid body with a strange image. Between two people, two big fish chase each other. The composition of the whole picture is free, very dynamic, simple and full of fantastic colors.

    Ancient Banpo people painted fish patterns and net patterns on many pottery pots, which should be related to totem worship and economic life at that time. Banpo people built settlements on the river valley terrace, lived a settled life dominated by agricultural production, and engaged in gathering, fishing and hunting. This kind of fish pattern decoration is a portrayal of their life.
