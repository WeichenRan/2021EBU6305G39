    Caie's sword

    • Dynasty: Modern
    • Category: Weapon
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Size: 93.2cm long

    Cai E (1882-1916) was born in Shaoyang, Hunan Province. He was an outstanding military strategist in the late Qing Dynasty and the early Republic of China. The commander in chief of the first army of the national protection army who opposed Yuan Shikai's claim to the emperor.

    On December 12, 1915, Yuan Shikai issued an order to accept the throne, which aroused opposition from people from all walks of life. Cai E, the former governor of Yunnan, and Li Liejun, a member of the Kuomintang, went to Kunming to contact Tang Jiyao, the governor of Yunnan, and sent a telegram against yuan on the 25th. The next year, Cai E led his troops into Sichuan and fought fiercely with Yuan Jun in Xuzhou (now Yibin), Luzhou, Qijiang and other places. The anti yuan campaign won popular support. Guizhou, Guangxi, Guangdong, Zhejiang, Shaanxi, Sichuan, Hunan and other provinces declared independence one after another, and the power was connected to promote yuan's resignation. Warlord bureaucrats of the northern system also alienated yuan, and imperialist countries "warned" yuan to suspend his claim to the emperor. Under internal and external pressure, yuan was forced to announce the abolition of the monarchy in March 1916 in an attempt to return to the presidency, but was rejected by all parties. In June, Yuan Shikai died of grief and anger. It is called "the war of protecting the country" in history.

    Cai E wore this knife during the war of national protection. The handle and scabbard of the knife are cast with one lion and eight little lions, all in the lying position, and the eyes of the big lion are precious stones. It is also engraved with flower patterns.
