    Kaiyuan banknotes

    • Dynasty: Tang
    • Category: Currency
    • Museum: multi Museum
    • Unearthed: multi places

    Kaiyuan Tongbao is a copper currency forged in the Tang Dynasty. In terms of quality, the general Kaiyuan Tongbao weighs one money per Wen, one or two per ten Wen, and six Jin and four Liang per penetration (i.e. one thousand Wen). Each Wen weighs two baht and four ounces, equivalent to about 4.2 grams. However, in the Kaiyuan period of Emperor Xuanzong of the Tang Dynasty, due to its prosperity, the copper material of Kaiyuan Tongbao increased by 10-20%. At this time, the mass of Kaiyuan Tongbao was about 4.5g-5g, so most of the heavy Kaiyuan Tongbao were cast at this time.

    In terms of style, Kaiyuan in the early Tang Dynasty had a bare back and no text. From the middle Tang Dynasty, there began to be stars, moon and other patterns on the back of the money, while Huichang Kaiyuan in the late Tang Dynasty added the place name of the money Bureau on the back of the money. After more than 300 years of casting, the edition is complex. After the Tang Dynasty, there were still smelting and casting, but most of the styles were different from those of the Tang Dynasty. In terms of specifications, Kaiyuan Tongbao is basically small flat money, but a small amount of "Kaiyuan Tongbao" was also cast in the Tang Dynasty, which is converted into ten big money, which is a commemoration of the opening of the furnace, and future generations also forged it.

    The casting and circulation of Kaiyuan Tongbao in the Tang Dynasty is of epoch-making significance in the development history of Chinese coin form. Kaiyuan Tongbao is abbreviated as Kaiyuan money or Tongbao money. Kaiyuan means "Founding the country and laying the foundation"; Tongbao is the connotation of "circulating treasure". The name of copper coin is Tongbao, which reflects people's further understanding of the role of money at that time. Taking money as treasure means the enhancement of the concept of money as treasure and the degree of people's worship of it.
