    Bronze cup

    • Dynasty: Xia
    • Category: bronze ware
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Unearthed: Yanshi County, Henan Province

    The device has a long flow and tail, no column, tied waist, flat bottom, hollowed out, and the three feet are in a thin cone shape. After testing, its alloy composition is 92% copper and 7% tin, belonging to tin bronze. It is the earliest bronze container in Chinese history. This bronze ware has thin wall, rough surface and no decoration, showing the characteristics of early bronze ware; However, it uses multiple composite molds for casting, and the process is complex. It is a leap in the history of ancient Chinese casting technology. Therefore, it has laid a technical foundation for the prosperity of bronze casting in the later Shang and Zhou dynasties.

    In addition, bronze sacrificial utensils such as Tomahawk, tripod, bronze cup and bronze drum also appeared in the Erlitou culture period. The inheritance and development of the later business and Zhou dynasties became the core of China's ancient bronze civilization.
