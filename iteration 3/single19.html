    Blue and white dragon pot

    • Dynasty: Ming
    • Category: porcelain
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Size: height 46cm, caliber 8cm

    The round mouth of the device is slightly turned out, the long neck is gradually widened from top to bottom, flat and round abdomen, oval and shallow circle feet. The white ground at the mouth and neck is painted with rolling grass pattern and tangled lotus pattern in blue and white, and the sea pattern on the abdomen is painted with blue and white. It is carved with two white dragons. Looking back and watching, the claws rise up and walk on the water. The waves roll, surge and roll up the white waves. It has the feeling of "startling waves on the shore and rolling up thousands of piles of snow", which further sets off the ferocity, boldness and arrogance of the swimming dragon.

    Cobalt blue glazed ground white flower decoration was first seen in the Jingdezhen kiln of the Yuan Dynasty. It was more skillfully produced in the Yongle and Xuande periods of the Ming Dynasty. The blue and white matched each other, reaching their own perfection, and the patterns were fresh and vivid. This ware is tall in shape, regular in production, and colorful in blue and white. It combines drawing and engraving decoration techniques. Its patterns are unrestrained and magnificent. It can be called a boutique of blue and white porcelain in the Ming Dynasty. The flat pot is a furnishing article. Its shape originates from the glass flat pot of Islamic culture in the 13th century, which reflects the cultural exchange and mutual influence between junior middle school and foreign countries in the Ming Dynasty.
