    iron plough

    • Dynasty: Han
    • Category: production tools
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Unearthed: Xianyang City, Shaanxi Province

    The iron plowshare is an isosceles triangle, with a front edge and an acute angle, with blades on both sides and a back edge. The plow wall is saddle shaped, and there is a protruding tip at the front end of the lower part, which can be inserted into the small hole on the share surface.

    The performance of plows in the Han Dynasty has become increasingly perfect. Among the unearthed objects, there are not only small plowshares that are light and convenient and suitable for cultivating mature land, but also large plowshares that are sharp and thick and suitable for opening up wasteland, as well as extra large plowshares used for ditching and making canals. According to different purposes, there are different specifications and shapes. The plow wall, also known as plow mirror and soil, is a major improvement in the structure of Chinese step plow. It has been unearthed in kind in Anqiu, Shandong, Hebi, Zhongmou, Xi'an, Longxian and Liquan, Shaanxi. The plow wall of Han Dynasty can be divided into diamond and saddle. The curved surface composed of plow wall and plowshare can break and turn over the cultivated soil ridge, and then rub it repeatedly, which can make the soil soft and the field surface flat, which is more conducive to the growth of plants. The area excavated from the plow wall is limited, and it has not appeared in the Han portrait stone so far, indicating that its use is not common.
