    Ming and Qing Periods

    The Ming Dynasty (1368-1644) was the last feudal dynasty founded by the Han Nationality in Chinese history. The Qing Dynasty (1636-1912), founded by the Manchus in the Far East, is widely regarded as the last feudal dynasty in China. The Ming and Qing dynasties are often called Ming and Qing Together. The Ming and Qing dynasties were a period of feudal society from prosperity to decline. During this period, imperial power was highly centralized, feudal autocratic centralization intensified, capitalism emerged and developed slowly, and ideology was strictly controlled.
