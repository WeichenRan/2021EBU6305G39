    Blue and white peony plate

    • Dynasty: Ming
    • Category: porcelain
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Size: height 8.6cm, caliber 45cm, foot diameter 26.5cm

    The disc is lotus petal type. The whole body is decorated with blue and white, the plate center is painted with broken branch Peony pattern, the inner and outer walls are painted with broken branch lotus pattern, and the inner and outer edges of the mouth are painted with water ripples. The outsole is flint red.

    There are few blue and white porcelain handed down from Hongwu official kiln. This plate is a typical artifact of Hongwu Period. It has regular production, rigorous pattern layout, concise and skilled painting, bold and vivid style and simple style. The composition of blue and white cobalt material of Hongwu Guan kiln is the same as that of Yuan blue and white, which are imported cobalt materials. However, most of the colors of Hong weapons are gray blue, which is not as bright and transparent as that of Yuan blue and white.
