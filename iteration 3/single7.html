    Yangling tiger Amulet

    • Dynasty: Qin
    • Category: bronze ware
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Unearthed: Lincheng County, Shandong Province

    This talisman is the certificate for the first emperor of Qin to mobilize the army. It is cast in bronze into a lying tiger shape, which can be divided into two. The left and right napes of the tiger have the same gold inlaid seal script inscription with 12 words: "the talisman of armour soldiers, right in the emperor and left in the Yangling." For this purpose, the military talisman is stored in the emperor's office on the right and the general's office stationed in Yangling (now the east of Xianyang City, Shaanxi Province) on the left; When the troops are mobilized, the envoys shall hold the right half to verify the conformity before it can take effect.

    Fu Jie is a kind of certificate used by the ancient Chinese court to convey orders, recruit soldiers and use them in various affairs. The earliest talismans seen at present are from the Warring States period.
