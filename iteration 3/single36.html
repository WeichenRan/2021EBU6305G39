    Hilltop cave Skull

    • Dynasty: Paleolithic Age
    • Category: bones
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Unearthed: Zhoukoudian site, Beijing

    The human fossils excavated from the mountaintop cave include 3 complete skulls, 3 pieces of residual skulls, 4 pieces of mandible, 3 pieces of mandibular fragments, dozens of sporadic teeth, several pieces of vertebrae, 1 segment of radius, 1 segment of femur, 1 segment of patella and 1 metatarsal. These fossil materials were lost in the Japanese invasion of China.

    The skull of mountaintop cave people is no different from that of modern people. Its brain volume is between 1300 ml and 1500 ml, which is in the variation of modern people's brain volume. Anthropologists regard mountaintop cave people as early yellow people.
