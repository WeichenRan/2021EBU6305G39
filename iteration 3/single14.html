    Agate plum bowl

    • Dynasty: Song
    • Category: daily necessities
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Material: agate

    The bowl is made of agate, with a six petal plum blossom shaped mouth, inlaid with Phnom Penh and arc-shaped wall. It is in the shape of six prism petals and small circle feet. The shape is elegant, the agate is exquisite and transparent, the light is plain and no grain, and the grinding is smooth and bright.

    This device takes the natural texture of agate as the pattern, which is gorgeous and beautiful, and has a high artistic level. In the Song Dynasty, the Ding kiln white porcelain was inlaid with gold mouth and copper mouth because the Ding kiln white porcelain was covered with fire and the mouth had no glaze. In order to increase the beauty of porcelain, gold mouth and copper mouth were inlaid. At present, the earliest jade with a gold mouth is the gold clasped jade cup unearthed from Li jingxun's tomb in the Sui Dynasty, which is noble and elegant. During the Tang and Song Dynasties, it was common to make cups and bowls with agate. Agate ware in the Tang Dynasty is very popular, such as the gold inlaid agate ox head cup and agate cup unearthed in Hejia village, Xi'an, Shaanxi, and the agate bowl unearthed from the Tang tomb in the eastern suburb of Xi'an. There are many kinds of agates. It is said that there are "thousands of agates and thousands of jade". The common colors include red, blue, yellow, brown, green, purple, powder and ink. Some of them are called flower agates. The texture is transparent or translucent.
