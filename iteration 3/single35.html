    Spotted deer fossil

    • Dynasty: Paleolithic Age
    • Category: fossil
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Unearthed: Zhoukoudian site, Beijing

    spotted deer and swollen bone deer, Chinese chimera hyena, Yang's tiger and other animals are unique types of Middle Pleistocene in North China. Their fossils have always been regarded as a standard stratigraphic fossil by geologists and paleontologists. That is, if these animal fossils are seen in the stratum, it can be basically confirmed that their age belongs to the Middle Pleistocene. Therefore, GE's spotted deer is not only the hunting object of Beijingers, but also a yardstick for dividing the times.

    Among the animal fossils unearthed at the beijingman site, there are few carnivores, and most of them are herbivores such as swollen bone deer and gerbil spotted deer. Few of these fossils are intact. Many of them have been burned and have been deformed and discolored. From then on, it is not difficult for us to infer that the diet of Beijingers is dominated by the meat of herbivores, and GE's spotted deer and swollen bone deer are their favorites. At the beginning, they didn't realize the delicacy of these two kinds of deer meat, but found that these two kinds of deer are relatively easy to catch, especially for hunters. The antlers can also be used as digging sticks, and the leftover deer bones can also be used as raw materials for fire.
