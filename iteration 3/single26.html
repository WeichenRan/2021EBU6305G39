    Textile carding machine

    • Dynasty: Qing
    • Category: production tools
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Size: 3.5m long, 2m wide and 1.5m high

    This textile carding machine is made of iron. The main part is a huge circular arch with a cotton feeding device for conveying cotton. It can transport the cotton rolls treated by the flower cleaning process to the pricking roller with metal wire brush on the surface for loosening, carding and impurity removal. All curly and flaky cotton rolls are combed into a basically straight single fiber state, and then gathered in the bell mouth, compressed into fiber strips by the pressing roller, and then tightly pressed by the ring strip pressing roller, The roll is stored in the cotton drum and transferred to the next process - drawing process. Carding is a very important process in the process of spinning cotton into yarn.

    This carding machine was originally one of a batch of textile machines ordered by Zhang Zhidong, governor of Huguang, from Yashi foreign bank, a German merchant. It was a product of Hetherington company in Manchester in 1895. Zhang Zhidong originally planned to use these machines to set up a textile factory in Wuchang, but failed. Later, Zhang Jian, the "number one industrialist", used them to set up Dasheng yarn factory. By the 1920s and 1930s, these machines were very old and should have been eliminated, but for various reasons, they incredibly tenaciously operated for more than 40 years, and were not eliminated until the late 1970s. This carding machine can be called the historical witness of the vicissitudes of China's national industry in the past century.
