    Jade Dragon

    • Dynasty: Paleolithic Age
    • Category: sacrificial vessels
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Unearthed: zuojiashan site, Jilin Province

    The organ is yellow and curly. There is a gap between the head and tail, which is not disconnected, and there is a large round hole in the center. The dragon's head is small, with ears on its head, raised eyes, wide snout and protruding mouth. The body is curled into a ring. The dragon's body is plain and has no decoration. A hole is crossed at the back of the neck, which can be tied. The ware is polished and glossy, with simple shape.

    Quti jade dragon, also known as "Jue" shaped dragon, is the largest kind of dragon shaped jade carving in Hongshan culture. Its shape and head are large and small. Some are disconnected at the gap, some are not disconnected, the times are early and late, and it is mostly located in the chest of the tomb owner when unearthed. The owner of the tomb should be a tribal leader or wizard with status and power. The jade dragon is a jade artifact used by wizards when they use jade as a God. This jade dragon is more primitive in shape than the jade pig dragon of Hongshan. Some archaeologists speculated from the stratigraphic accumulation that it may be earlier than the Hongshan Culture in Liaoning.
