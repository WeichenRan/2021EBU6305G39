    Dream Pool Essays

    • Dynasty: Song
    • Category: Books
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Author: Shen Kuo

    Dream Pool Essays author Shen Kuo (1031-1095 AD), from Qiantang, Hangzhou (now Hangzhou, Zhejiang Province), is a famous scientist in the Northern Song Dynasty. When he served as the supervisor of heaven, he wrote to the emperor to improve the astronomical instruments such as the armillary sphere, floating leak and landscape watch, and advocated replacing the traditional yin-yang calendar with the Gregorian calendar. In mathematics, he founded "gap product technique" (summation method of equal difference series) and "circle meeting technique" (knowing the diameter of circle and the height of bow, and the method of calculating the chord and arc length of bow). In physics, he discovered geomagnetic declination, scientifically expounded the principle of concave mirror imaging, and studied the law of resonance. In addition, Shen Kuo has made many achievements in geology, medicine, temperament, paleontology and so on.

    In his later years, Shen Kuo settled in Mengxi garden in Runzhou (now Zhenjiang City, Jiangsu Province) and wrote a book about his life experience and screenwriter's dream model. In addition to politics, military system and humanities, about 40% of the book is about astronomy, meteorology, mathematics, physics, chemistry, biology, geology, medicine, engineering technology and other natural sciences. The book summarizes the achievements of Natural Science in ancient China, especially in the Northern Song Dynasty, contains the contributions of some "little people" to science and technology, and also records Shen Kuo's own ideas. It is a precious material for describing China's ancient cultural heritage.
