    railway surveyor

    • Dynasty: Modern
    • Category: production tools
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Size: 38cm long and 33.5cm wide

    Zhan Tianyou (1861-1919), a native of Nanhai, Guangdong, with the word "Xuncheng". In 1872, he was admitted to the preparatory class for children to go abroad to study in the United States. In 1881, he graduated from the Department of civil engineering of Yale University and returned home to become China's first railway engineer. In 1908, he served as the chief engineer of Beijing Zhangjiakou Railway and the meeting office. He overcame many difficulties and built China's first railway completely relying on his own strength in only four years. This surveying and mapping instrument was used by Zhan Tianyou when he built the Beijing Zhangjiakou Railway.
