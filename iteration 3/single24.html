    The Scholars

    • Dynasty: Qing
    • Category: Books
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Author: Wu Jingzi

    The external history of the scholars is a novel written by Wu Jingzi in the Qing Dynasty. It was written in the 14th year of Qianlong (1749) or a little ago. It is now handed down in manuscript and first engraved in the 8th year of Jiaqing (1803).

    The fifty-six chapters of the book depict the different manifestations of "fame and wealth" by all kinds of people with realism. On the one hand, it truly reveals the process and reasons for the corrosion of human nature, so as to deeply criticize and ridicule the corruption of officials, the disadvantages of imperial examination and the hypocrisy of ethics at that time; On the one hand, it enthusiastically praises the protection of human nature made by a few characters in the way of adhering to themselves, so as to embody the author's ideal. The use of vernacular in the novel has become proficient and free, and the characterization of characters is also quite in-depth and delicate. In particular, the superb satirical technique has made the book a masterpiece of Chinese Classical Satirical Literature.

    The Scholars represents the peak of Chinese ancient satirical novels. It creates an example of directly evaluating real life with novels. A manuscript of the book was handed down after it was published, which was highly praised by later generations. Lu Xun believed that the ideological content of the book was "fair minded and criticized the disadvantages of the times", and Hu Shi believed that its artistic characteristics could be called "Seiko refining". In the International Sinological circles, the book has a great influence. It has been handed down in English, French, German, Russian, Japanese, Spanish and other languages for a long time, and has been highly praised by the Sinological circles. Some people believe that the foreign history of Confucianism can be ranked among the world's literary masterpieces, can be compared with the works of Boccaccio, Cervantes, Balzac or Dickens, and is an outstanding contribution to world literature.
