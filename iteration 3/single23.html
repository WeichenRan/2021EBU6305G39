    Taiping Kingdom Coin

    • Dynasty: Qing
    • Category: Currency
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Size: diameter 11.3cm, weight 626g

    The face text "Taiping Heavenly Kingdom", read in pairs. The coins of the Taiping Heavenly Kingdom were first cast in 1853. After the Taiping Heavenly Kingdom established its capital in Tianjing (now Nanjing), the casting organization was called "coin casting yamen". The coins were cast and circulated in the areas controlled by the Taiping Heavenly Kingdom. In addition, there are non circulating town Treasury money and big money. Zhenku money is extra large and overweight money cast before the opening of circulating coins, which is used as a way for Qianku to suppress evil. Big money is a commemorative coin specially forged during major celebrations of the Taiping Heavenly Kingdom as a gift to meritorious officials and foreigners.

    The Taiping Heavenly Kingdom uprising (1851-1864 AD) was a major event in the history of the late Qing Dynasty. The uprising lasted 14 years and spanned more than half of China. It once approached the heart of the rule of the Qing Dynasty, causing great panic among the rulers. More importantly, the Taiping Heavenly Kingdom occupied the affluent land in the south of the Yangtze River, which was "the best wealth in the world". On the one hand, the local social productivity was seriously damaged, which exacerbated the decline of the Qing Dynasty; On the other hand, the fiscal revenue of the Qing government was hit hard, so that after the war, it had to rely on the customs controlled by foreigners to increase the tax revenue, which made the Qing government more dependent on the foreign powers and sold the national rights and interests.
