    longevity embroideries

    • Dynasty: Han
    • Category: Silk
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Unearthed: Changsha City, Hunan Province

    The textile industry in the Western Han Dynasty was developed, especially silk production. It had advanced technology, large output and many varieties. The official silk workshop has a large production scale and can weave a variety of exquisite and gorgeous silk fabrics. In addition to being used at home, it is also exported abroad, which provides a material basis for the prosperity and trade exchanges of the "Silk Road" across Asia and Europe. A large number of silk fabrics such as "cloud embroidery" yellow brown silk unearthed from the Western Han tomb in Mawangdui, Changsha, include most of the known fabrics of the Han Dynasty. They have complete chromatography, bright patterns and sophisticated production technology. They are rare treasures in archaeological excavations and fully reflect the high achievements of the silk weaving technology of the Han Dynasty.
