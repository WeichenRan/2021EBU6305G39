    Yuanpu side sail scroll

    • Dynasty: Qing
    • Category: painting
    • Museum: National Museum of China
    • Author: Yong Yu

    This picture is painted by Yongyu for Qianlong. It mainly shows the scene near the river. The whole picture is divided into three sections. Unfold the volume: near the picture, there are sparse wooden hills and grass covering the empty Pavilion; The sand dunes in the water are faint, and the old man fishing alone; The Rocks rise suddenly, and the accumulated stones are stacked; The hut is on the roof, and the ancient pine breaks the rock; The river revolves between stones, on the Gaoshi Xuhang bridge; It is close to the mountain and close to the water under the rock. One minute in the water, I saw the fisherman holding the pole. Mountains rise again after water, ups and downs; Several boats race in the water, and the shore and mountain are far away; The river is circuitous and the Qingxi river is winding; The temple is secluded in the forest, and the pagoda is hanging high. Fishing boats moor in the Bay, with wide water; The distant mountains are faint and the water and sky are the same. This picture takes the view of "Pingyuan" and expands the picture one by one with "scattered perspective", with rich changes. The picture shows the structure of Zhongshan stone with "Shama Cun" and "folded belt Cun", and decorated with "alum head". The pen is rigorous and many yuan people's pen and ink. The color of the whole picture is desolate, and the artistic conception is cold and lonely.

    At the end of the picture, Yongyu said: "minister Yongjing painting." The lower seal is printed with "Zi ChenYong" in white and "Jing Hua" in Zhu Wenfang.
